Why Institutional Readiness Matters

Many energy and clean-technology projects fail not because of technology, but because they enter institutional and regulatory environments unprepared.

Common failure points include unclear ownership and influence structures, blurred boundaries between R&D and deployment, legacy arrangements incompatible with EU expectations, and premature engagement with institutions.

MyHorizon¬≤ addresses these gaps before they become structural blockers.

What We Do

We provide strategic advisory services focused on energy-related initiatives where governance, compliance and institutional acceptance are critical.

Energy Project Positioning & Structuring

We support project sponsors in defining clear and defensible project positioning from an institutional and regulatory perspective.

This includes clarifying project scope, distinguishing between R&D, pilot and deployment phases, mapping institutional relevance, and avoiding category mismatches that create regulatory or political risk.

Compliance & Governance Readiness

We prepare projects for external scrutiny by identifying governance and compliance gaps before formal engagement with institutions, investors or advisors.

Our work covers ownership and control mapping, governance structure review, identification of red-flag issues, and preparation of internal compliance narratives suitable for EU-level contexts.

EU Institutional & Policy Interface

We advise on how and when to approach European institutions, and how to align project logic with institutional expectations.

This includes orientation between relevant EU Directorates, engagement sequencing, preparation of institution-ready positioning notes, and sensitivity to political and reputational considerations. We do not act as lobbyists.

Cross-Border & Transitional Advisory

Many energy initiatives operate across EU and non-EU environments or carry legacy structures incompatible with current regulatory frameworks.

We support projects in designing EU-compatible structures, separating legacy elements from forward-looking initiatives, and managing complex internal transitions.

Typical Engagements

Engagements are typically exploratory, time-bounded and focused on clarity and sequencing.
